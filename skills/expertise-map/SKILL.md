# Expertise Map - Knowledge and Skills Assessment

**Name:** Expertise Map  
**Description:** Maps the client's areas of expertise across primary skills, secondary skills, emerging knowledge, and industry understanding to guide content creation and determine authoritative speaking topics.

## What This Skill Does

The Expertise Map skill creates a comprehensive assessment of the client's knowledge areas, skill levels, and confidence ratings across different domains. This map serves as a strategic guide for content creation, ensuring the AI Employee only speaks authoritatively on topics where the client has genuine expertise while identifying opportunities for thought leadership and learning.

Beyond just listing skills, this map captures the depth of knowledge, real-world application experience, and confidence levels that determine how the AI should approach different topics. It prevents overreach while maximizing the client's credibility and expertise positioning.

## How to Collect Information

### Primary Expertise Assessment

#### Core Professional Skills
1. What would you consider your top 3-5 areas of greatest expertise?
2. In what areas are you recognized as an expert by peers or clients?
3. What topics could you teach a masterclass on today?
4. What areas do clients specifically seek you out for?
5. Where do colleagues and competitors ask for your insights?

#### Depth and Experience Evaluation
For each primary expertise area:
1. How many years of hands-on experience do you have?
2. What results or outcomes demonstrate your expertise?
3. Have you implemented this knowledge in multiple contexts/organizations?
4. Do you have formal credentials or certifications in this area?
5. Have you taught, spoken, or written about this topic?

### Secondary Skills Inventory

#### Supporting Competencies
1. What skills complement your primary expertise?
2. What areas are you knowledgeable in but wouldn't call yourself an expert?
3. What topics can you discuss intelligently but might defer to others for deep technical details?
4. What skills have you developed through necessity rather than specialization?

#### Confidence and Application
For each secondary skill:
1. How comfortable would you be discussing this topic publicly?
2. In what contexts have you applied this knowledge?
3. What's the gap between your current knowledge and true expertise?
4. Would you feel confident advising others in this area?

### Emerging Knowledge Areas

#### Learning and Development
1. What new skills or knowledge areas are you actively developing?
2. What topics are you curious about but haven't mastered yet?
3. What industry trends are you studying or experimenting with?
4. What skills do you plan to develop over the next 1-2 years?

#### Innovation and Experimentation
1. What new methodologies, tools, or approaches are you testing?
2. Where are you pushing the boundaries of your current expertise?
3. What controversial or emerging viewpoints are you exploring?
4. What knowledge gaps are you actively working to fill?

### Industry and Market Knowledge

#### Market Understanding
1. How well do you understand your industry's competitive landscape?
2. What market trends have you observed firsthand?
3. How familiar are you with regulatory or compliance issues in your field?
4. What economic factors affecting your industry do you understand well?

#### Cross-Industry Insights
1. What industries outside your primary field do you understand well?
2. What transferable principles have you learned from other sectors?
3. Where do you see connections between different industries or disciplines?

## How to Use in Daily Operations

### Content Planning and Creation
1. **Topic Selection:** Only create authoritative content in 7+ confidence areas
2. **Collaboration Decisions:** Partner with others for 4-6 confidence areas
3. **Learning Content:** Share journey and questions for 1-3 emerging areas
4. **Authority Positioning:** Lead conversations in primary expertise areas

### Speaking and Media Opportunities
1. **Accept Speaking Requests:** For topics rated 7+ with relevant experience
2. **Suggest Alternative Topics:** When requests fall in secondary skill areas
3. **Decline Appropriately:** For topics below confidence threshold
4. **Propose Panel Participation:** For collaborative topics

### Business Development and Positioning
1. **Lead with Strengths:** Emphasize primary expertise areas in all marketing
2. **Supporting Credentials:** Use secondary skills to round out capabilities
3. **Future Positioning:** Communicate emerging expertise development
4. **Honest Boundaries:** Clearly define scope of expertise and limitations

### Learning and Development Priority
1. **Skill Gap Analysis:** Focus development on highest business impact areas
2. **Authority Building:** Invest in strengthening near-expert areas (6-7 range)
3. **Market Relevance:** Develop emerging skills that enhance market position
4. **Continuous Assessment:** Regular review and adjustment of expertise map

## Success Criteria

1. Comprehensive mapping of all knowledge areas with accurate confidence levels
2. Clear guidelines for content creation based on expertise levels  
3. Strategic development plan for emerging knowledge areas
4. Honest assessment that prevents overreach while maximizing authority
5. Regular updates reflect skill development and changing expertise
6. Business alignment between expertise development and market opportunities

This skill provides the foundation for authentic authority building and strategic content creation by mapping real knowledge and capabilities while identifying growth opportunities.
