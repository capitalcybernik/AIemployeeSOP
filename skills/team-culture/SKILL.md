# Team Culture - Company Culture & Internal Identity

**Name:** Team Culture  
**Description:** Documents comprehensive company culture including core values, mission statement, team traditions, communication style, celebrations, internal language, leadership philosophy, and hiring criteria for authentic content that reflects real culture.

## What This Skill Does

The Team Culture skill captures the authentic internal culture of the organization, going beyond generic corporate messaging to document how the team actually operates, communicates, and lives their values. This includes core values in practice, mission and purpose, team traditions, communication patterns, what the team celebrates, internal language and terminology, leadership philosophy, and hiring criteria.

The AI Employee uses this information to ensure all content reflects the real company culture rather than generic corporate speak, making communications more authentic and relatable to both team members and external audiences.

## How to Collect Information

### Discovery Questions for Client

#### Core Values & Mission
1. What are your stated core values, and how do you actually live them daily?
2. What's your mission statement, and how does it show up in everyday decisions?
3. What principles guide how you treat employees, customers, and partners?
4. What behaviors do you reward and recognize?
5. What would cause someone to be a poor culture fit?

#### Communication Style
1. How formal or casual is your internal communication?
2. What's your communication style with clients (professional, friendly, direct)?
3. Do you use specific terminology or jargon that's unique to your organization?
4. How do you handle disagreements or conflicts?
5. What communication channels do you use most (Slack, email, in-person)?

#### Team Dynamics
1. How would you describe the energy and atmosphere in your workplace?
2. What traditions, rituals, or regular activities bring the team together?
3. How do you celebrate wins, milestones, or achievements?
4. What's your approach to work-life balance?
5. How do you support team growth and development?

#### Leadership Philosophy
1. How would you describe your leadership style?
2. What's your approach to decision-making (collaborative, directive, consensus)?
3. How do you handle mistakes and learning opportunities?
4. What's your philosophy on employee autonomy and accountability?
5. How do you recognize and develop talent?

#### Hiring & Growth
1. What personality traits or characteristics do you look for in new hires?
2. What questions reveal whether someone will fit your culture?
3. What would be red flags in the hiring process?
4. How do you onboard new team members into the culture?
5. What does career growth look like in your organization?

## How to Use in Daily Operations

### Content Creation
1. Use authentic language and terminology from culture documentation
2. Reference real values and behaviors rather than generic corporate speak
3. Include cultural elements that make content more relatable and human
4. Ensure all external communication reflects authentic internal culture

### Hiring and Onboarding
1. Use cultural fit criteria during candidate evaluation
2. Reference culture documentation during onboarding process
3. Help new team members understand unwritten cultural norms
4. Use culture as framework for performance discussions

### Team Development
1. Reference cultural values when giving feedback
2. Use culture documentation to resolve conflicts or misunderstandings
3. Align growth opportunities with cultural values
4. Celebrate behaviors that exemplify cultural values

### Client Relationships
1. Communicate in ways that reflect authentic team culture
2. Make decisions that align with documented values
3. Use cultural strength as differentiator in sales process
4. Ensure service delivery reflects cultural commitments

## Example Output

```markdown
# Team Culture - Creative Digital Agency

**Last Updated:** March 7, 2025
**Culture Maturity:** Established
**Team Size:** 12 people

## Culture Overview

### Mission & Purpose
**Official Mission:** "We help ambitious businesses tell their stories in ways that matter"

**Mission in Practice:** Every project starts with understanding not just what the client does, but why it matters and who it serves. We turn down projects that don't align with this.

**Why We Exist:** Too many good businesses struggle to communicate their value clearly. We bridge that gap.

**Impact We Want:** A world where authentic businesses can compete with flashy marketing by telling better, truer stories.

---

## Core Values

### Craft Over Speed
**Definition:** We take the time to do things right rather than rushing to finish first

**In Practice:** 
- We never send first drafts to clients
- Every piece gets reviewed by at least two team members
- We build buffer time into all project schedules for refinement

**Recognition:** Monthly "Craft Award" for work that exemplifies attention to detail

**Red Flags:** Rushing work, skipping quality checks, "good enough" mentality

### Curiosity Over Assumptions
**Definition:** We ask questions instead of making assumptions about clients, markets, or solutions

**In Practice:**
- Every project starts with discovery questions, not solutions
- We research industries we haven't worked in before taking on clients
- Team members regularly share interesting articles and insights

**Recognition:** "Question of the Month" shared in team meetings

**Red Flags:** Assuming we know the answer, copying previous solutions without adaptation

---

## Communication Culture

### Internal Communication Style
**Tone:** Friendly but focused
**Formality Level:** Low - we use first names and casual language
**Decision Speed:** Deliberate - we discuss before deciding
**Feedback Approach:** Direct but kind - we tell truth with care

### Communication Principles
- Default to transparency: Share information unless there's a reason not to
- Assume positive intent: When someone disagrees, assume they care about the outcome
- Speak up early: Don't wait until problems become crises

---

## Team Traditions & Rituals

### Regular Celebrations
**Weekly:** Friday afternoon show-and-tell where anyone can share something interesting
**Monthly:** Team lunch at a restaurant none of us have tried before
**Quarterly:** Half-day creative workshops where we learn new skills together

### Milestone Recognition
**Project Completions:** Client success stories shared with whole team, not just account manager
**Personal Milestones:** Handwritten notes from leadership for birthdays and work anniversaries
**Team Achievements:** When we hit quarterly goals, everyone gets to leave 2 hours early on Friday
```

## Success Criteria

1. Comprehensive documentation of authentic culture (not aspirational culture)
2. Clear connection between stated values and daily behaviors
3. Specific language and terminology that reflects real team communication
4. Practical hiring and onboarding guidance based on cultural fit
5. Cultural elements that differentiate the organization authentically
6. Living document that evolves with the team but maintains core identity
7. Content that reflects genuine culture rather than generic corporate messaging

This cultural documentation ensures the AI Employee can create authentic content that reflects the real personality and values of the organization, making all communications more genuine and effective.
