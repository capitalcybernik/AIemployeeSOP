# Visual Brand Guidelines - Brand Standards & Creative Direction

**Name:** Visual Brand Guidelines  
**Description:** Documents comprehensive visual brand standards including colors, fonts, logo usage, photography style, icon style, social media templates, and creative do's and don'ts for consistent brand application across all materials.

## What This Skill Does

The Visual Brand Guidelines skill creates a comprehensive reference for all visual brand elements that ensures consistent brand application across every touchpoint. This includes color palettes with exact hex codes, typography specifications, logo usage rules, photography guidelines, icon styles, social media templates, and detailed do's and don'ts for creative applications.

The AI Employee uses this information when creating content briefs, specifying design requirements, or providing creative direction to ensure all visual content maintains brand consistency and professional quality.

## How to Collect Information

### Discovery Questions for Client

#### Logo & Brand Mark
1. Do you have a primary logo and any variations (horizontal, stacked, icon-only)?
2. What formats do you have (PNG, SVG, EPS, etc.)?
3. What are the minimum size requirements for logo usage?
4. Are there any backgrounds or contexts where the logo doesn't work?
5. Do you have specific clear space requirements around the logo?

#### Color System
1. What are your primary brand colors (with hex codes if available)?
2. Do you have secondary or accent colors?
3. What colors should never be used with your brand?
4. How do your colors work in different contexts (print, digital, light/dark backgrounds)?
5. Do you have approved color combinations or palettes?

#### Typography
1. What fonts do you use for headers, body text, and accents?
2. Do you have both paid and free font alternatives?
3. What font weights and styles are approved?
4. How should typography hierarchy work in your materials?
5. Are there any fonts that should never be used?

#### Photography & Imagery
1. What style of photography represents your brand (corporate, casual, artistic)?
2. Do you prefer people in images? What type of people?
3. What mood should your images convey?
4. Are there specific lighting, color, or composition preferences?
5. What type of imagery should be avoided?

#### Design Elements
1. Do you use specific icon styles or illustration approaches?
2. What graphic elements or patterns are part of your brand?
3. How do you handle borders, shadows, gradients, or effects?
4. What layout principles guide your design decisions?
5. Do you have templates for common materials?

## How to Use in Daily Operations

### Content Brief Creation
1. Reference visual guidelines when creating briefs for designers
2. Specify exact colors, fonts, and style requirements
3. Include brand personality and mood requirements
4. Provide templates and examples from the guidelines

### Creative Review Process
1. Use visual guidelines as checklist for reviewing creative work
2. Ensure compliance with color, typography, and spacing standards
3. Check logo usage against established rules
4. Verify photography style matches brand requirements

### Platform Content Creation
1. Apply platform-specific guidelines for social media content
2. Use approved templates for consistent posting
3. Maintain color and typography standards across platforms
4. Ensure all visual content supports overall brand cohesion

### Vendor Management
1. Provide visual guidelines to external designers and agencies
2. Use guidelines for creative briefings and project requirements
3. Establish quality standards for deliverables
4. Create approval processes based on brand compliance

## Success Criteria

1. Comprehensive documentation of all visual brand elements
2. Exact specifications (colors, fonts, sizes) that eliminate guesswork
3. Clear usage rules that prevent brand misapplication
4. Platform-specific guidelines for consistent brand presence
5. Template systems that streamline content creation
6. Quality standards that maintain professional brand image
7. Regular updates to keep guidelines current and relevant

This visual brand system ensures the AI Employee can provide accurate creative direction and maintain consistent brand application across all visual touchpoints.
