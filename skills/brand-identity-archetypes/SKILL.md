# Brand Identity Archetypes - Brand Personality Mapping

**Name:** Brand Identity Archetypes  
**Description:** Maps the client's brand to established psychological archetypes to create consistent brand personality and messaging that resonates with target audiences.

## What This Skill Does

The Brand Identity Archetypes skill identifies and documents which of the 12 universal brand archetypes (Hero, Sage, Innocent, Explorer, Outlaw, Magician, Regular Person, Lover, Jester, Caregiver, Creator, Ruler) best represents the client's brand personality. It maps primary and secondary archetypes, defines how they show up in messaging, and establishes the emotional connections the brand should create.

This creates a psychological framework for all brand communication, ensuring consistent personality across all touchpoints and helping the AI Employee make strategic messaging decisions that align with deep human motivations and desires.

## How to Collect Information

### Brand Personality Discovery Questions

#### Core Identity
1. If your brand were a person at a party, how would they behave?
2. What values are absolutely non-negotiable for your brand?
3. What change do you want to create in the world?
4. What motivates you to do this work beyond money?
5. What would your brand never say or do?

#### Customer Relationship Style
1. Are you the expert guide or the reliable friend to your customers?
2. Do you challenge customers to grow or comfort them through difficulties?
3. Are you disrupting an industry or perfecting existing solutions?
4. Do customers come to you for innovation or stability?

#### Brand Personality Traits
1. Is your brand more rebellious or traditional?
2. More nurturing or challenging?
3. More exclusive or inclusive?
4. More fun and playful or serious and focused?
5. More pioneering or mastery-focused?

#### Competitive Positioning
1. How do competitors in your space typically present themselves?
2. What personality gap exists in your market?
3. What archetype do your ideal customers aspire to be?
4. What emotional need are you fulfilling that others miss?

#### Communication Style Preferences
1. Do you prefer to educate, inspire, challenge, or comfort in your messaging?
2. Should your brand sound authoritative or collaborative?
3. Do you lead with logic or emotion?
4. Are you more direct or nuanced in communication?

## How to Use in Daily Operations

### Content Strategy Development
1. Check primary archetype before planning content themes
2. Ensure all content ladders up to core motivation
3. Use archetype-aligned story structures and messaging
4. Reference emotional positioning for campaign planning

### Message Development Process
1. Start with archetype core message as foundation
2. Apply archetype communication style to all copy
3. Check that tone and language match archetype personality
4. Ensure customer journey emotions align with archetype promise

### Brand Decision Making
1. Use archetype as filter for strategic decisions
2. Ask "Would [Primary Archetype] do this?"
3. Evaluate partnerships and collaborations through archetype lens
4. Apply archetype test to new product/service development

### Crisis Communication
1. Respond in a way that reinforces archetype strength
2. Address challenges using archetype strategies
3. Maintain personality even under pressure
4. Use crisis as opportunity to demonstrate archetype values

```markdown
# Jennifer Walsh Consulting - Brand Identity Archetypes

**Created:** March 7, 2025
**Last Updated:** March 7, 2025
**Assessment Method:** Client interview + content analysis

## Brand Archetype Summary

### Primary Archetype: The Sage
**Strength:** 9/10 alignment score
**Core Motivation:** To understand and share knowledge that helps others make better decisions
**Brand Promise:** Deep expertise delivered with clarity and wisdom

### Secondary Archetype: The Caregiver
**Strength:** 7/10 alignment score
**Supporting Role:** Adds nurturing and supportive elements to expertise-driven approach

## Your Brand Archetype Profile

### Primary Archetype Deep Dive

**Archetype:** The Sage
**Alignment Score:** 9/10
**Customer Promise:** Trusted expertise that cuts through confusion and provides clear direction

#### Core Characteristics
- **Motivation:** Helping others understand complex business challenges
- **Fear:** Being wrong or misleading clients with bad advice
- **Strategy:** Mastery through continuous learning and teaching others
- **Gift:** Wisdom that transforms confusion into clarity

#### Brand Personality Traits
- Knowledgeable yet approachable
- Thoughtful and deliberate
- Intellectually curious
- Humble about expertise
- Patient teacher
- Evidence-based decision maker

#### Communication Style
- **Tone:** Authoritative but warm, professorial without being condescending
- **Language:** Clear, precise, avoids jargon, explains complex concepts simply
- **Message Focus:** Education, insight, strategic thinking, long-term perspective

### Secondary Archetype Integration

**Archetype:** The Caregiver
**Supporting Role:** Softens the pure expertise with genuine care for client success
**When to Emphasize:** During onboarding, problem-solving, and difficult transitions

## Brand Messaging Framework

### Core Brand Messages

#### Primary Message (The Sage)
"I help business leaders cut through complexity to make strategic decisions with confidence and clarity."

#### Supporting Messages (The Caregiver)
"Your success matters to me, and I'm here to support you through every challenge."

### Emotional Positioning

#### Target Emotions
**Primary:** Confidence and Clarity
**Secondary:** Support and Trust

#### Customer Journey Emotions
- **Awareness:** Relief (finally, someone who really understands)
- **Consideration:** Trust (this person clearly knows what they're talking about)
- **Purchase:** Confidence (I'm making the right choice)
- **Experience:** Clarity (everything makes sense now)
- **Advocacy:** Gratitude (they transformed how I think about my business)

## Content Applications

### Content Themes Aligned to Archetype
- Strategic frameworks and models
- Industry analysis and trends
- Case studies with deep insights
- "How to think about..." educational content
- Myth-busting and clarifying misconceptions
- Long-form thought leadership
- Behind-the-scenes decision-making processes

### Story Types That Resonate
- Client transformation stories (Caregiver)
- Industry insights from years of experience (Sage)
- "What I learned from..." reflective pieces
- Strategic pivots and their outcomes
- Frameworks that solved complex problems

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Emphasize Primary Archetype (Sage)
- Thought leadership content
- Strategic consulting discussions
- Industry speaking opportunities
- Educational webinars and workshops

### When to Lean into Secondary Archetype (Caregiver)
- Client onboarding and check-ins
- During client difficulties or setbacks
- Testimonials and case studies
- Community building and networking

### What to Avoid
- Being preachy or know-it-all
- Rushing to solutions without understanding
- Dismissing others' perspectives
- Overwhelming with too much information at once
```

4. Brand differentiation is clear in competitive landscape
5. Content strategy flows naturally from archetype framework
6. Team members can make brand-consistent decisions using archetype guide
