# Voice Architecture - Platform-Specific Communication Strategy

**Name:** Voice Architecture  
**Description:** Defines how the brand voice adapts across platforms and contexts including LinkedIn vs Twitter tone, formality levels per channel, emoji usage, hashtag strategy, and call-to-action styles to ensure consistent but contextually appropriate communication.

## What This Skill Does

The Voice Architecture skill creates a comprehensive framework for how brand voice adapts across different platforms, audiences, and contexts while maintaining core brand identity. This goes beyond a single "brand voice" to document how communication style should shift between LinkedIn and Twitter, formal vs casual contexts, email vs social media, and different audience segments.

The AI Employee uses this information to ensure all communications are both consistent with brand identity and contextually appropriate for the specific platform, audience, and situation.

## How to Collect Information

### Discovery Questions for Client

#### Platform Usage & Strategy
1. Which platforms do you actively use for business communication?
2. What's the purpose of each platform in your overall strategy?
3. How formal or casual should your communication be on each platform?
4. What type of content performs best for you on each platform?
5. Which platforms generate the most engagement or business results?

#### Audience Segmentation
1. Do you communicate differently with prospects vs existing clients?
2. How does your tone change based on audience seniority (C-level vs manager level)?
3. Do you adjust communication style based on industry or company size?
4. How do you communicate differently in one-on-one vs group contexts?
5. What communication style do your best clients prefer?

#### Voice Adaptation Rules
1. When should your communication be more formal vs casual?
2. How do you handle humor, emoji, and informal language?
3. What's your approach to hashtags, mentions, and platform-specific features?
4. How do you adjust calls-to-action for different contexts?
5. What topics require more careful or formal communication?

#### Content Context
1. How should your voice differ between educational vs promotional content?
2. What's your approach to thought leadership vs company news?
3. How do you handle sensitive topics or crisis communication?
4. What's your style for responding to comments and engaging with others?
5. How do you balance personality with professionalism?

## How to Use in Daily Operations

### Content Creation
1. Reference platform-specific guidelines before creating any content
2. Adjust formality level and tone based on platform and audience
3. Use appropriate emoji, hashtag, and formatting rules for each platform
4. Ensure CTAs match the platform's communication style

### Social Media Management
1. Apply voice architecture consistently across all social platforms
2. Tailor engagement style to match platform norms and audience expectations
3. Use platform-specific templates for common responses and interactions
4. Maintain brand voice while adapting to platform culture

### Email Communication
1. Adjust email voice based on recipient relationship and context
2. Use appropriate formality levels for different types of email communication

1. Use voice architecture to train team members on brand communication

```markdown
# Voice Architecture - Digital Marketing Agency

**Last Updated:** March 7, 2025
**Voice Maturity:** Established
**Platforms Covered:** 8

## Core Voice Foundation

### Universal Brand Voice Traits
**Personality Adjectives:** Knowledgeable, approachable, results-focused, authentic
**Communication Values:** Clarity over cleverness, helpful over salesy, human over corporate
**Voice Consistency:** Always lead with value, speak in plain English, show genuine enthusiasm for client success

---

## Platform-Specific Voice Guidelines

### LinkedIn

#### Voice Profile
**Tone:** Professional thought leader with approachable expertise
**Formality Level:** Professional but conversational
**Personality Emphasis:** Knowledge and results-focus
**Content Focus:** Industry insights, client results, business strategy

#### Communication Style
**Post Structure:** Hook + insight + takeaway + question
**Language Usage:** Business professional with some casual phrases
**Industry Jargon:** Use sparingly, always explain when used
**Personal Touch:** Share wins, lessons learned, behind-scenes insights

#### Hashtag Strategy
**Number of Hashtags:** 3-5 hashtags
**Hashtag Types:** Mix of industry (#digitalmarketing), niche (#B2Bmarketing), and branded (#MarketingResults)
**Hashtag Placement:** In comments, not in post body

#### Call-to-Action Style
**Professional CTAs:** "What's been your experience with X?"
**Networking CTAs:** "I'd love to hear your thoughts in the comments"
**Business CTAs:** "If this resonates, let's continue the conversation"

---

### Twitter/X

#### Voice Profile
**Tone:** Quick insights with personality
**Formality Level:** Casual but smart
**Personality Emphasis:** Approachable and helpful
**Content Focus:** Quick tips, industry commentary, real-time reactions

#### Communication Style
**Tweet Structure:** Lead with value, keep it scannable
**Language Usage:** More casual, contractions okay
**Humor Usage:** Light humor about marketing challenges, self-deprecating
**Real-time Reactions:** Jump into marketing conversations with helpful insights

#### Hashtag Strategy
**Number of Hashtags:** 1-3 hashtags maximum
**Trending Hashtags:** Only join if genuinely relevant to expertise
**Branded Hashtags:** Use sparingly, focus on value hashtags
```
