# Voice DNA Profile - Writing Style Analysis

**Name:** Voice DNA Profile  
**Description:** Analyzes the client's existing content to extract their unique writing voice, communication patterns, and linguistic preferences for consistent AI-generated content.

## What This Skill Does

The Voice DNA Profile skill dissects the client's existing written content to identify and document their unique communication style. It analyzes patterns in sentence structure, vocabulary choices, humor style, formality levels, punctuation habits, and recurring phrases to create a comprehensive voice reference guide.

This profile becomes the foundation for all AI-generated content, ensuring the AI Employee writes in the client's authentic voice rather than a generic AI style. It captures the subtle nuances that make communication feel genuinely personal and on-brand.

## How to Collect Information

### Content Gathering Interview Questions

#### Existing Content Sources
1. Can you provide 10-15 recent emails you've sent to clients or prospects?
2. Do you have blog posts, articles, or thought leadership pieces you've written?
3. Can you share your social media posts from the last 3 months (LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.)?
4. Do you have any sales pages, website copy, or marketing materials you wrote?
5. Can you provide presentation transcripts or speaking notes?
6. Do you have any personal writing (newsletters, personal blog, journal entries you're comfortable sharing)?

#### Style Preferences
1. Do you prefer formal or casual communication in business settings?
2. Are there specific words or phrases you love using?
3. Are there words or phrases you avoid or dislike?
4. How do you usually start emails or messages?
5. What's your typical closing style?
6. Do you use humor in business communication? What type?
7. How technical do you get when explaining your expertise?
8. Do you prefer short, punchy sentences or longer, detailed explanations?

#### Brand Voice Guidelines
1. If your brand were a person, how would they talk?
2. What tone do you want to avoid sounding like?
3. How do you want clients to feel after reading your communication?
4. Are there any industry voices or writing styles you admire?

## How to Use in Daily Operations

### Before Writing Any Content
1. Open VOICE-DNA.md and review the voice summary
2. Check the specific content type guidelines (email, social, blog, etc.)
3. Reference the DO/DON'T lists for word choices
4. Apply their typical sentence structure patterns

### Content Creation Process
1. Write the first draft in the client's voice using their patterns
2. Review against the voice characteristics checklist
3. Adjust formality level to match their score
4. Incorporate their favorite phrases naturally
5. Remove any words from the "avoid" list
6. Match their typical content length and structure

### Quality Control
1. Read the content aloud in the client's "voice"
2. Check if the humor level and style match their profile
3. Verify punctuation matches their patterns
4. Ensure vocabulary sophistication is appropriate
5. Confirm the content "sounds like them"

### Voice Evolution
1. Save new client-written content to analyze for voice changes
2. Update the profile monthly with new patterns
3. Note any intentional voice evolution or brand shifts
4. Maintain consistency while allowing for natural growth

## Example Output

```markdown
# Marcus Chen - Voice DNA Profile

**Created:** March 7, 2025
**Content Sources:** 12 client emails, 8 LinkedIn posts, 3 blog articles, website copy
**Last Updated:** March 7, 2025

## Voice Summary
Marcus writes with confident expertise but maintains approachability through conversational language and strategic humor. He balances data-driven insights with human storytelling, creating content that feels both authoritative and relatable.

## Core Voice Characteristics

### Formality Level
**Scale:** 1-10 (where 1 = very casual, 10 = very formal)
**Score:** 6/10
**Notes:** Professional but conversational, uses contractions, avoids overly corporate language

### Sentence Structure
- **Average Length:** 18 words
- **Complexity:** Moderate with varied structure
- **Patterns:** Mixes short punchy statements with longer explanatory sentences

### Vocabulary Preferences
**Sophistication Level:** Professional with accessible explanations
**Industry Jargon Usage:** Moderate, always explains technical terms
**Common Power Words:** "Transform," "strategic," "breakthrough," "proven," "game-changer"

### Punctuation Style
- **Exclamation Points:** Occasional, used for genuine excitement
- **Em Dashes:** Never uses in prose, prefers commas or periods
- **Ellipses:** Rare, only in casual contexts
- **Question Marks:** Often uses rhetorical questions to engage readers

## Signature Elements

### Favorite Phrases
- "Here's the thing..."
- "The reality is..."
- "I've seen this firsthand"
- "Let's be honest"
- "The bottom line?"

### Word Preferences
**Uses Instead Of:**
- "Clients" → "Customers" or "Partners"
- "Leverage" → "Use" or "Apply"
- "Synergy" → Never uses corporate buzzwords

### Words to Avoid
- "Utilize" (prefers "use")
- "Ecosystem" 
- "Paradigm shift"
- Any unnecessary corporate jargon

### Humor Style
**Type:** Self-deprecating and observational
**Frequency:** 2-3 times per longer piece
**Context:** Used to make complex topics more relatable, never at others' expense

## Communication Patterns

### Email Style
- **Opening:** "Hope you're doing well" or direct topic dive
- **Body Structure:** Problem → Solution → Next steps
- **Closing:** "Let me know if you have questions" + "Best, Marcus"
- **Length:** 100-200 words, concise but complete

### Social Media Voice
- **Platform Variations:** More casual on Twitter, professional storytelling on LinkedIn
- **Hashtag Usage:** Light, 2-3 relevant hashtags maximum
- **Emoji Usage:** Occasional, mostly professional (📈📊💡)

## Voice Guidelines for AI

### DO Use:
- Conversational tone with professional expertise
- Data points backed by human stories
- Strategic questions to engage thinking
- Marcus's signature phrases naturally
- Confident but humble language

### DON'T Use:
- Corporate buzzwords or jargon
- Overly casual slang
- Aggressive sales language
- Industry acronyms without explanation

### Sentence Structure Rules:
- Vary between 10-25 words
- Start some sentences with "But" or "And" for flow
- Use active voice predominantly
- Include one rhetorical question per paragraph when appropriate

### Tone Guidelines:
- Expert advisor, not pushy salesperson
- Helpful teacher, not condescending professor
- Confident professional, not arrogant authority
```

## Success Criteria

1. AI-generated content is indistinguishable from client's natural writing
2. Voice profile captures both obvious and subtle communication patterns
3. Content consistently feels authentic to the client's brand
4. Clients say "This sounds exactly like me"
5. Voice evolution is tracked and updated regularly

This skill ensures every piece of AI-generated content maintains the client's authentic voice and communication style.
